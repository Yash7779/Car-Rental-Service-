Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How old do I need to be to rent a car?

Typically, the minimum age to rent a car is 25. However, some rental companies may allow individuals aged 21-24 to rent with an additional young driver surcharge.

What documents do I need to rent a car?

Generally, you will need a valid driver's license, a credit card in your name, and sometimes an additional form of identification. International renters may need a passport and an international driver's license.

What is the difference between basic and comprehensive insurance coverage?

Basic insurance usually covers damage to the rental car, while comprehensive insurance may include coverage for third-party liability, medical expenses, and personal property. It's essential to understand the extent of coverage before renting.

Can I add additional drivers to my rental agreement?

Yes, many rental companies allow you to add one or more additional drivers to the rental agreement. However, there may be an extra fee associated with adding extra drivers.

Is there a mileage limit on the rental vehicle?

Rental agreements often come with a daily mileage limit, and exceeding this limit may result in additional charges. Some rentals offer unlimited mileage, so it's crucial to clarify this before booking.

What happens if I return the car late?

Late returns can result in additional charges. It's essential to be aware of the rental company's policies regarding late returns and whether there's a grace period.

How do I pay for tolls during my rental period?

Some rental companies offer electronic toll payment options, while others may provide toll transponders. Alternatively, you may need to pay tolls separately or use your own electronic toll device.

Can I modify or cancel my reservation, and what are the associated fees?

Rental companies often allow modifications or cancellations, but fees may apply. It's crucial to understand the cancellation policy and any associated charges before making a reservation.
